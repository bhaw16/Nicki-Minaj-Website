Nicki Minaj Albums

Pink Friday

  1. Pink Friday is Nicki's debut studio album and it was released on Novemeber 22nd, 2010. It is loaded with hip hop and pop songs. Some songs are about Nicki's triumphs and emotions, such as "I'm the Best," "Fly," "Right Thru Me" and "Moment 4 Life." Others are fun songs to dance to such as "Super Bass" and "Check it Out," which was written by both Nicki and will.i.am.
  2. The cover art depicts one of Nicki Minaj's alter egos, Nicki the Harujuku Barbie (often referred to as Barbie). Barbie is one of Nicki's most famous alter egos alongside Roman Zolanski (The two alter egos were arguing in Nicki's feature on Kanye West's song Monster). Roman is also on the song "Roman's Revenge," which is one of the songs from the Pink Friday album. There are two versions: one with a feature from Eminem and one with a feature from Lil Wayne. Roman makes frequent appearances on Nicki's next album, "Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded."

Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded

  1. Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded is Nicki Minaj's second studio album and was released on April 2nd, 2012. It is a mix of hip-hop and dance-pop songs, allowing Nicki to be free and have fun as a follow up to the Pink Friday album.
  2. The most notable songs on the album are "Roman Holiday," (which depicts Roman Zolanski, one of Minaj's most iconic alter egos) "Starships" (the lead single), "Pound the Alarm," "Va Va Voom," "Beez in the Trap" and "Right by My Side." The latter 5 songs listed are the singles used to promote the album.

Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded - The Re-Up

  1. Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded - The Re-Up is a reissue of Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded and was released on November 19th, 2012 (7 months after the original). It features seven newly recorded songs and an exclusive behind the scenes footage DVD.
  2. The album's three signles "The Boys," "Freedom" and "High School" peaked at #41, #31 and #20, respectively, on the US Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart, and the latter peaked at #64 on the Billboard Hot 100. The album was also promoted through the Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded Tour and Nicki's 2012 AMAs performance of "Freedom."

The Pinkprint

  1. The Pinkprint is Nicki's 3rd studio album which was released on December 15th, 2014. The most popular song from the album is "Anaconda," and the music video for that song has about a billion views. This song showcases the fierce and confident Nicki that everyone knows, which some people criticize her for and think that she is overrated and untalented. However, many of the sings oin the Pinkprint shows a sentimental side of Nicki, where she sings and raps about emotional experiences.
  2. Songs like "Grand Piano" (one of my favorite Nicki songs of all time and one of the most emotional songs from the Pinkprint; it's the final track on the regular edition of the album), "Pills N Potions," "All Things Go" and "I Lied" (another emotional song), Nicki showed that she's not just another sexual rapper, and that she uses her music to express her feelings like a true artist.

"Only" features Drake and Chris Brown, where Nicki denies having relationships with certain rappers in the industry. Queen

  1. Queen is Nicki's fourth studio album, and it was released on August 10th, 2018. It is her first studio album in almost 4 years, the last one being thePinkprint. It was promoted on Queen Radio on Apple Music, where she "tells it like it is." While many people don't like Queen as much as her other albums, it is considered as an iconic comback by the Barbz, and many of the songs are praised and love, showing off Nicki's talent and parts of herself from past eras.
  2. This is shown in songs like "Barbie Dreams," which is based on one of the songs from one of her mixtapes when she first started out, and she uses many funny lines when dissing the rappers (which is done all in good fun, as many of the rappers she disses are her colleagues and friends).
  3. In addition, Nicki Minaj's "Chun-Li" features her alter ego of the same name, which she created based on the video game character of the same name, which is almost like a metaphor, as Chun-Li is the first prominent female fighting game character and Nicki is considered to have revitalized female rap. This is because many rappers before her are not as active as she is now, such as Lil Kim and Queen Latifa. Creating alter egos is also something that Nicki is known for, and it is her way of applying what she learned about acting at LaGuardia to her music.
  4. Many of the songs on this albums are her asserting her dominance and showing that she is the MVP of the rap game, and reminding everyone of her status as the Queen of Rap. Examples of such songs are "Chun-Li" and "LLC." The song also features more sassy songs that portary Nicki as what I call a "baddie," such as "Good Form," which has a version with only Nicki and another version that features Lil Wayne, and "Chun Swae," which features Swae Lee. This album all in all shows the confident Nicki that everyone is familiar with, and she is making sure that everyone knows that she won't retire her crown anytime soon.
